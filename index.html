Canadian Investment Tax Guide

Understand CRA tax rules, optimize investment tax burdens, and avoid tax risks

Stock Investment Taxation

When investing in stocks through non-registered accounts, 50% of capital gains from sales must be reported. Earnings within a TFSA are tax-free, while withdrawals from an RRSP are taxed at the personal income tax rate.

Cryptocurrency Taxation

The CRA stipulates that cryptocurrency trading, mining, and staking earnings must all be reported. Trading gains are treated as capital gains, while mining/staking earnings are treated as business income.

Annual Filing Deadline

The deadline for personal tax filing is April 30 each year, and June 15 for self-employed individuals. Any outstanding taxes must be paid by April 30.
